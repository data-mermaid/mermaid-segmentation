# Team Process

This page explains how we coordinate, communicate, and stay aligned as a team.

---

## Before starting any work

**Check the GitHub project board first.** Priorities were set during planning and are the source of truth for what to work on. Starting something that isn't tracked or assigned can duplicate effort or conflict with someone else's work.

**Don't self-assign an issue without checking with the team lead.** If you see something you'd like to work on, bring it up in the Monday meeting or message the team lead in Slack before starting.

**If something feels urgent or out of scope, raise it** — at the Monday meeting or in Slack. Don't start work on it unilaterally.

**Review the following resources** :

- [How to Contribute to Open Source](https://opensource.guide/how-to-contribute/)
- [Version Control with Git](https://swcarpentry.github.io/git-novice/)
- [Programming with Python](https://swcarpentry.github.io/python-novice-inflammation/)
- [The Missing Semester of Your CS Education](https://missing.csail.mit.edu)

## Regular rhythms

### Monday meetings

We meet every Monday to discuss priorities, review progress, and surface blockers. Attend unless you've let the team know in advance that you can't.

This is also the right forum to propose changes to priorities or scope. Plans were agreed upon upfront — if you think something should shift, bring it here rather than improvising.

### Weekly Slack bot

A Slack bot sends a weekly check-in to the team. **Respond to it every week.** It takes two minutes and is how the team stays synchronized without needing a second meeting.

### Slack updates

Share progress in Slack proactively. A short "working on X, hit a snag with Y" message keeps teammates informed and opens the door for help. Don't wait to be asked.

---

## Expectations

These apply to everyone on the project:

- **Respond to the weekly Slack bot check-in** — every week, no exceptions
- **Attend Monday meetings** — or let the team know in advance if you can't
- **Update colleagues in Slack regularly** — proactively, not reactively, about progress, blockers
- **Ask for help early.** If you've been stuck for more than a day, post in Slack. There are no bad questions, and staying quiet while blocked slows everyone down.
- **Follow the agreed-upon plan.** Bring proposed changes to the Monday meeting rather than improvising.

---

## When to escalate

- **Blocked for more than a day**: post in Slack
- **Scope or priority question**: raise it at the Monday meeting
- **AWS/S3 access issue**: post in Slack with the exact error — this requires action from the team lead
- **Interpersonal concern**: if reasonable, try to bring up concerns with the person directly. If that
does not resolve the issue then please reach out to the team lead directly.

---

## GitHub project board

The project board is the canonical view of what's in progress, what's up next, and what's done. Check it before you start anything new. If your work isn't on the board, it shouldn't be happening yet.
